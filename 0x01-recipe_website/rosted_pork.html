Slow Roasted Pork Shoulder With Apple Gravy

On a chilly winter day, this slow-cooked pork shoulder is the ideal meal. It overnights in the marinade before roasting all day. When the food is fall-apart tender, dinner is done. Serve with some roast veggies on the side and a simple apple gravy.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme or 2 teaspoons packed, fresh thyme leaves, gently chopped
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme or 2 teaspoons packed, fresh thyme leaves, gently chopped
  • 1 tablespoon roasted fennel seeds
  • two tablespoons of black pepper
  • kosher salt, two tablespoons
  • 4 medium garlic cloves, minced
  • optional extra virgin olive oil

    • For the roast pork shoulder

  • 4 to 5 lbs (1.8 to 2.2 kg) Pork shoulder that is boneless, sinew-free, and fattier than 1/4 inch trimmed
  • 4 medium-quality cooking apples, such as Jonagold or Fuji
  • 1 medium onion, yellow
  • Olive oil, two tablespoons
  • To taste, add salt and pepper.
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) (120 ml) stale white wine (can sub water)
  • a half-teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • black pepper freshly ground

Method of Cook

  1. Creating the spice rub
  2. Roast should spend the night in a spice rub.
  3. Prepare apple and onion
  4. Set the oven to 450°F (232°C) before using.
  5. Place the roast over an apple and onion bed.
  6. Roast
  7. Create sauce
  8. Serve
